# PMKSY – Per Drop More Crop (PDMC): Central Operational Guidelines

Document ID: PMKSY-PDMC-CENTRAL
Issuing authority: Department of Agriculture, Cooperation & Farmers Welfare (DAC&FW), Government of India
Scheme component: Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana – Per Drop More Crop (Micro Irrigation)

## 1. Objective

Per Drop More Crop (PDMC) is the micro-irrigation component of the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY). Its objective is to enhance water use efficiency at the farm level through precision irrigation technologies, primarily drip and sprinkler (micro-sprinkler) systems. The component promotes the principle of "more crop per drop" by delivering water directly to the root zone of the plant, thereby reducing conveyance and evaporation losses.

## 2. Subsidy pattern (assistance to farmers)

Under the central guidelines, financial assistance for the installation of micro-irrigation systems is provided as follows:

- Small and Marginal Farmers: 55% of the indicative unit cost.
- Other Farmers (medium and large landholdings): 45% of the indicative unit cost.

The indicative unit cost is fixed by a national committee and revised periodically. The subsidy is calculated on the indicative unit cost or the actual cost of installation, whichever is lower.

A farmer is eligible for assistance for a maximum command area of 5 hectares per beneficiary during the currency of the scheme. Assistance for the same land parcel is admissible once every seven years for drip systems and once every ten years for sprinkler systems.

## 3. Cost-sharing between Centre and State

The scheme is implemented on a cost-sharing basis. For most States, the Centre and State share the subsidy burden in the ratio 60:40. For North-Eastern States and Himalayan States, the ratio is 90:10 in favour of the Centre. For Union Territories, the Centre bears 100% of the cost.

## 4. Eligibility

All categories of farmers are eligible provided they:

- hold cultivable land in their name or hold a registered long-term lease of not less than seven years;
- have an assured source of irrigation (borewell, dug well, canal outlet, farm pond, or community water source);
- have not availed micro-irrigation subsidy for the same land parcel within the applicable cooling-off period (seven years for drip, ten years for sprinkler).

Aadhaar seeding of the beneficiary bank account is mandatory for Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT).

## 5. Approved system components

Assistance covers the drip mainline, sub-mains, laterals, emitters/drippers, filters (screen, sand, or hydrocyclone as required), fertigation equipment (venturi or fertilizer tank), control valves, and pressure gauges. Pumping units and water sources are NOT covered under PDMC unless bundled with a solar pump scheme under a separate convergence provision.

## 6. Implementation and release of funds

State Governments implement the scheme through a designated State Implementing Agency (SIA). Funds are released by the Centre to the State Nodal Account in two instalments. Beneficiary subsidy is credited directly to the farmer's Aadhaar-seeded bank account through DBT after physical verification of the installed system by a third-party inspection agency.

## 7. Monitoring

Every installation must be geo-tagged with GPS coordinates and photographed at the time of physical verification. A unique work-order ID is generated for each beneficiary. States are required to upload beneficiary and installation data to the central Management Information System (MIS) portal on a monthly basis.
