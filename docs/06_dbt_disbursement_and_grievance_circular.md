# Operational Circular: DBT Disbursement Timelines and Grievance Redressal for Micro-Irrigation Subsidy

Document ID: MI-DBT-GRIEVANCE
Issuing authority: Micro-Irrigation Mission, Programme Management Unit
Document type: Operational circular

## 1. Purpose

This circular standardises the timelines for subsidy disbursement, the grievance-redressal mechanism, and the documentation trail for the micro-irrigation subsidy programme. It applies to all installations processed under the Per Drop More Crop component and associated State top-ups.

## 2. Disbursement timeline

The standard end-to-end timeline from installation to subsidy credit is as follows:

- Physical verification by the inspection agency: within 15 days of the farmer reporting completion of installation.
- Upload of verification report and geo-tagged photographs to the MIS: within 3 days of verification.
- Sanction and generation of the payment advice: within 7 days of report upload.
- Credit of subsidy to the beneficiary account (DBT): within 10 days of sanction.

The total standard timeline should therefore not exceed 35 days from reported completion to subsidy credit. Any case exceeding 45 days is automatically flagged as a delayed case in the MIS and escalated to the State Nodal Officer.

## 3. Where subsidy is credited

For States following the Direct Benefit Transfer model, the subsidy is credited to the farmer's Aadhaar-seeded bank account. For States following a supplier-credit (nodal-agency-pays-supplier) model, the subsidy is credited to the empanelled supplier against the specific work-order, and the farmer's contribution is the only amount the farmer pays. The circular recognises both models as valid depending on the State's implementation architecture.

## 3A. Documentation trail

Every case must maintain: the application ID, the sanctioned work-order ID, the pre-installation and post-installation geo-tagged photographs, the third-party verification report, the supplier invoice, and the DBT transaction reference number. Records must be retained for a minimum of seven years for audit.

## 4. Grievance redressal

A three-tier grievance mechanism is prescribed:

- Tier 1: Taluka / Block Agriculture Officer — first point of contact, resolution target 15 days.
- Tier 2: District Superintending Agriculture Officer — for cases unresolved at Tier 1, resolution target 21 days.
- Tier 3: State Nodal Officer of the Micro-Irrigation Mission — final State-level authority, resolution target 30 days.

Grievances may be filed on the State farmer portal or through the central PMKSY grievance portal. Every grievance is assigned a unique ticket ID and its status is trackable by the farmer.

## 5. Common grievance categories

The most frequently reported grievances are: (a) delay in subsidy credit beyond the standard timeline, (b) discrepancy between the sanctioned and credited subsidy amount, (c) poor quality of installed equipment or emitter clogging within the warranty period, (d) non-empanelled or unresponsive supplier, and (e) rejection of application without a recorded reason.

## 6. Quality and warranty

Empanelled suppliers must provide a minimum warranty of three years on the drip system, covering manufacturing defects in laterals, emitters, and filters. Warranty claims are routed through the Tier-1 grievance channel. Repeated verified quality complaints against a supplier can result in de-empanelment by the State nodal agency.
