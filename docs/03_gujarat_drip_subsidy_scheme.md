# Gujarat Green Revolution Company (GGRC) Micro-Irrigation Scheme

Document ID: GJ-MICRO-IRR-STATE
Issuing authority: Gujarat Green Revolution Company Ltd. (GGRC), Government of Gujarat
Applicable region: State of Gujarat

## 1. Overview

Gujarat implements micro-irrigation through a single nodal agency model. The Gujarat Green Revolution Company Ltd. (GGRC) is the sole State-designated nodal agency for the procurement, subsidy administration, and monitoring of drip and sprinkler systems in the State. This single-window model differs from the multi-agency, taluka-officer model used in some other States.

## 2. Effective subsidy

The effective subsidy under the GGRC model, inclusive of the central PMKSY component and the State share, is:

- Small and Marginal Farmers: 70% of the system cost.
- Other Farmers: 60% of the system cost.

In addition, farmers who install drip systems for the first time on previously un-irrigated land receive an extra one-time incentive of 10% of the system cost, capped at Rs. 15,000, credited as a "new adopter" bonus. This new-adopter bonus is unique to Gujarat and is not part of the central scheme.

## 3. Maximum area

Gujarat permits assistance for a maximum command area of 10 hectares per beneficiary, which is higher than the central ceiling. The additional area between 5 and 10 hectares is funded entirely from the State's own budget without central contribution.

## 4. Nodal agency and procurement

Under the GGRC model, the farmer selects an empanelled micro-irrigation system supplier from the GGRC portal. GGRC directly pays the subsidy portion to the supplier and the farmer pays only the balance (the farmer's contribution). This is a "supplier-credit" model. In contrast to Direct Benefit Transfer to the farmer's account, the subsidy in Gujarat flows to the supplier on behalf of the farmer.

## 5. Cooling-off period

Gujarat allows a farmer to avail micro-irrigation subsidy for the same land parcel once every five years for drip systems. This is shorter than the central seven-year cooling-off period; the difference for years five to seven is borne by the State.

## 6. Application process

Applications are made online on the GGRC i-Khedut linked portal. Required documents are the 7/12 and 8-A land records, Aadhaar, bank details, and a passport photograph. There is no lottery; applications are processed on a first-come-first-served basis until the annual budget is exhausted.

## 7. Water-source condition

Gujarat does not mandate a separate groundwater certification for large parcels. A self-declaration of the water source by the farmer, counter-signed by the empanelled supplier during the technical survey, is sufficient. This is a lighter documentation requirement than in some neighbouring States.
