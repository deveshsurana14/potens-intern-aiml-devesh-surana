# Maharashtra State Micro-Irrigation Subsidy Scheme (Mukhyamantri Shashwat Krishi Sinchan Yojana Top-Up)

Document ID: MH-MICRO-IRR-STATE
Issuing authority: Department of Agriculture, Government of Maharashtra
Applicable region: State of Maharashtra

## 1. Overview

Maharashtra implements the central PMKSY–Per Drop More Crop component and provides an additional State top-up subsidy over and above the central assistance. The combined objective is to raise the effective subsidy available to farmers in the State's drought-prone and rain-shadow districts (notably Vidarbha and Marathwada) and to accelerate drip adoption for horticulture and sugarcane.

## 2. Effective subsidy after State top-up

After adding the State top-up to the central PDMC assistance, the effective subsidy for Maharashtra farmers is:

- Small and Marginal Farmers: 80% of the indicative unit cost.
- Other Farmers: 75% of the indicative unit cost.

The State top-up is credited as a second DBT transaction after the central component has been disbursed. The top-up is available only to farmers in the notified drought-prone talukas; farmers in non-drought talukas receive the central rates only (55% and 45%).

## 3. Priority crops and zones

Priority for the State top-up is given, in order, to: (1) horticulture and orchard crops, (2) sugarcane, (3) cotton, and (4) other field crops. Within the State, the Vidarbha and Marathwada divisions receive first claim on the annual State top-up allocation.

## 4. Maximum area

The State follows the central ceiling of 5 hectares per beneficiary. However, for Farmer Producer Organisations (FPOs) registered in Maharashtra, the aggregated ceiling is 25 hectares across member farmers, subject to individual member limits.

## 5. Application process

Applications are submitted through the Maharashtra "Mahadbt" farmer portal. A farmer must first register on Mahadbt, upload the 7/12 land extract, Aadhaar, and bank passbook, and then apply under the micro-irrigation component. Lottery-based selection is used where applications in a taluka exceed the sanctioned target for the year.

## 6. Verification

Installation is verified by the Taluka Agriculture Officer along with a representative of the empanelled micro-irrigation company. Geo-tagged photographs are mandatory. The State top-up is released only after the central component verification is complete.

## 7. Water-source condition

Unlike the central guideline, Maharashtra requires that the applicant demonstrate an assured perennial water source certified by the local Groundwater Surveys and Development Agency (GSDA) for drip installations above 2 hectares. Applications without GSDA certification for large parcels are held in abeyance.
